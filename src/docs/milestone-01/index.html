Overview

At the University of Massachusetts Amherst, booking rooms for things like music rehearsals, group studies, and private sessions is a hassle right now. It's confusing and frustrating for users. Our web app solves this problem by putting all room bookings in one easy-to-use platform. Now, the UMass community can reserve spaces with no trouble. This centralized system streamlines the booking process and ensures fair access to campus facilities. It addresses the scattered booking issue, greatly benefiting students, faculty, and staff, and paving the way for a more organized and efficient campus.

Centralizing room bookings can dramatically improve how campus spaces are used. It cuts down on the time spent managing reservations and reduces conflicts over room allocations. The system makes room availability transparent, so everyone can plan their activities ahead of time more easily. Additional benefits include better utilization of campus resources and more satisfied users overall.

Application Parts

User Authentication

A crucial component of the application is the user authentication and authorization system. This ensures that only authorized users can access the appropriate resources and functionalities within the application. The schema includes fields such as user_id, email, password_hash, role, first_name, and last_name, which store user information and credentials. The application will likely have mechanisms for user registration, login, password management, and role-based access control. This component is critical for maintaining data privacy, security, and providing personalized experiences to different user groups.

Building and Room Management

Another essential component is the management of buildings and rooms. The schema includes tables for building and room, with fields like name, address, latitude, longitude, room_number, capacity, and description. This component will handle the creation, updating, and deletion of buildings and rooms within the application. It may also encompass functionalities like searching for available rooms based on specific criteria, displaying room details, and potentially integrating with mapping services for location-based features.

Reservation System

The reservation system is a crucial component that ties together the user and room management aspects of the application. The reservation table in the schema includes fields like reservation_id, user_id, room_id, start_time, end_time, num_people, and is_using_computer. This component will allow users to browse available rooms, make reservations for specific time slots, specify the number of attendees, and indicate whether they require computer resources. The reservation system may also include features like managing conflicts, sending reminders, and providing a user-friendly interface for viewing and modifying existing reservations.

Data Requirements

At the heart of our room reservation system lies the cautious management of critical data types. Each category of data we collect and process is foundational to delivering a service that is not only efficient and user-friendly but also secure and adaptable to the diverse needs of our user base. From ensuring the security of user accounts through authentication data to facilitating easy and precise room searches with comprehensive room information, our focus on data integrity is paramount. Furthermore, we understand the importance of reservation data in maintaining a conflict-free scheduling environment and leverage user preferences and history to offer a tailored booking experience.

The first type of data we handle is user authentication data. This includes email addresses and hashed passwords. Authentication data ensures that each user's account is secure and that they can access only the reservations and functionalities appropriate to their role, whether student, faculty, or administrator. Students can search for and book rooms for study groups or club meetings, view their upcoming reservations, and modify or cancel their own bookings. Faculty members have the added ability to reserve rooms for classes and academic events, access reservation histories, and override certain booking restrictions based on their needs. Administrators possess the highest level of access, including managing room availability, approving or denying reservation requests that fall outside normal parameters, and modifying user roles to maintain the integrity and efficiency of the reservation process. Different permissions ensure a smooth and efficient operation of the reservation system, tailored to the specific needs and responsibilities of each user group.

Next, we manage room information. This includes details about each room available for reservation, such as location, capacity, available dates and times, and technical equipment available (like computers and projectors). This up-to-date room data allows users to effectively search and find spaces that meet their specific requirements. Additionally, users are able to search based on specific attributes like the building in which the room is located or the closest room location, further simplifying the process of identifying the ideal space for their requirements. We handle this feature by storing the building data and geographical coordinates for each room in our database.

Another crucial type of data is reservation data. This includes information about who has booked a room, what room is booked, at what times, and number of people. Reservation data is key to preventing double bookings and ensuring that users can plan their activities without conflicts.

We also prioritize user preferences and history to facilitate our autofill functionality. This data helps streamline the booking process by predicting and filling in reservation forms with information based on past user behavior and saved preferences, significantly reducing the time and effort required to make a booking. Initially, our application will autofill reservation forms with previously entered user data. Looking ahead, we plan to augment this feature with Natural Language Processing (NLP) to further refine and personalize the user experience.

In conclusion, our application relies on carefully managing and protecting diverse types of data to provide a user-friendly, efficient, and secure room reservation service. By focusing on these data essentials, we aim to enhance the academic and extracurricular life on campus.

Wireframes

Below is the home page that aims to have the users start using our website right away. A search bar and a collection of room cards are immediately accessible, offering details such as location and real-time availability which we find most people would care about when booking a room. There is also a location-based sorting feature provides a practical benefit, dynamically ordering room options according to their distance from the user.

The sign-in page has a simple and straightforward design. There are features such as "stay logged in" and "forgot password" which can make it more convenient for the users. The registration form focuses on collecting the standard data typically required for on-campus room reservations. We can store the information in the database and use it to autofill future forms, which will save users' time.

The reservation form is all about making things quick and easy for the users. As soon as the user lands on the page, there will be a big 'Reserve' button right at the top so the users don't have to scroll down and click when their information is already filled out. The user's details will be stored in our database, and the form will automatically fill in stuff like name, email address, major, etc. Autofilling is the main feature of this project and hopefully using this, people at UMass, especially those are new, will no longer have to look around multiple websites to reserve the room the want.

Real-World Connection

Filling the same forms on different websites have been a tedious task for everyone. One typical example of this is booking flights, where users previously had to visit multiple airline websites and fill out the same information repeatedly. Companies such as Expedia or Google Flights addressed this hassle by compiling forms into a single website, which helps save people's time significantly. Another example would be filling out job applications, which follows the same form structure for pretty much all jobs. Simplify helps save applicants' time by letting the students only fill out their information once and automatically fill out the application for them every time they apply.

Similarly, we found navigating different websites to book rooms in UMass and input the same information over and over again frustrating. This problem has been something a lot of our friends have also experienced. We often book rooms to do group study in the UMass and the process of finding the website and fill out the same form every time is just tedious.

This is why we decided to build UMass Room Reserve website as a final project. By gathering all the forms together and having the users fill out only once, the website will save time for students and faculty at UMass, much like the flight booking platforms streamlined travel arrangements by offering a single point of access for multiple airlines.
